― Support With a Bequest ―



What is a bequest?



A "bequest" refers to the transfer of all or part of a property


to a specific individual or group free of charge upon the death


of the owner by way of a will left during his or her lifetime.


A bequest donation is a donation made through that system.


Even if an heir donates


from the inherited property, it may be included as a "bequest".




About bequest donation



We accept small amounts.


Any amount of bequest or inheritance donation is accepted.

When it cames to a beguest, some people imagine

a large amount of money, but there is no fixed amount.

Some people put their life first while they are alive,

and only a portion of their last remaining assets, such as

"100,000 yen" or "10% of their financial assets," is bequeathed.

We would appreciate it if you could consider supporting us within a reasonable range.



We also accept assets other than cash.


We also other accept assets including real estate such as land and houses,

securities such as stocks, precious metals, and paintings.

As a general rule, non-cash items will be exchanged for cash.

However, it may not be accepted depending on the assets and contents of the will.



Inheritance tax is not levied on bequests.


A receipt will be issued for the bequest,

so when filing an inheritance tax return by attaching the receipt

to the inheritance tax declaration,

the bequeathed property will be deducted from the amount of property included in inheritance tax calculation.




Please feel free to contact us if you have any questions.

About food support



Minna no Ouchi also accepts food donations.


We are also working on reducing food loss and increasing local production


for local consumption, all with the support of local people.


Please feel free to contact us if you have any food items


that are salvageable but cannot be sold due to their shape or other appearance.
